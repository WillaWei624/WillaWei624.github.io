The problem

Separation anxiety is common, but the apps to solve it are rare

Separation anxiety between pets and their owners is a growing emotional challenge — especially for young urban professionals who live alone with their pets. Through my research, I found that 90% of surveyed pet owners experienced some level of anxiety when separated from their pets, yet very few apps specifically address this need.

Existing apps either focus on pet transportation (complex, expensive, limited to cats and dogs) or offer AI companionship with a 10-minute free cap. Neither solution helps owners feel genuinely connected and in control when away.

Persona

Designing for Grace Li

I synthesized research into a primary persona: Grace Li, 25, a game character designer living alone in Shanghai with two cats. Her demanding work schedule often forces late nights and business trips — leaving her anxious about her cats and unable to focus on work. She wants to stay connected with her pets, access emotional support, and ultimately achieve a healthier work-life balance.

Design process

From paper sketches to high-fidelity

After mapping the user journey — tracing Grace's emotional path from leaving home to reunion — I moved through a structured design process, validating at each stage.

1
Ideation
Brainstorming & information architecture
Generated ideas across 4 opportunity areas using FigJam sticky notes. Prioritised features based on user pain points, then structured the full IA — from app open through all 4 main sections: Fostering Recommendation, Controlling Remotely, Community, and Self (profile/settings).
2
Sketching
Paper sketches — rapid exploration
Hand-drew multiple layout options for each screen: Home, Monitor, Toy/Feed/Mood, Pet Sitting, and Community. Paper sketches allowed fast iteration before committing to digital wireframes.
3
Prototyping
Lo-fi prototype & early user feedback
Built grayscale wireframes in Figma for core flows: homepage, monitor, mood tracker, pet sitting, and community. Tested with users to validate the concept and collect early feedback before adding visual design.
4
Visual design
Design system & high-fidelity UI
Established a warm, pet-friendly design system: Open Sans typography, a golden primary palette (#FFE786 → #F8CB4E), mood colours for anxiety/calm/happy states, and a full iconography set. Applied to all final screens.
5
Validation
Usability testing
Conducted usability testing with participants testing key features. All participants completed tasks successfully and found the app helpful for reducing separation anxiety.
Final design

Five features, one connected experience

Homepage
Real-time connection at a glance
The homepage shows travel duration, pet's current mood, daily feeding progress, and a photo/video memory feed — all giving owners a sense of presence even when physically apart. Login supports email and social accounts.
Remote Control
Toys & feeders, controlled from anywhere
Owners watch live video from different rooms, remotely control interactive toys (mice toy, fish toy, plush ball), manage automatic feeders, and see when their pet last interacted with toys and food — closing the distance gap that existing apps leave open.
Mood Tracking
Understand your pet's emotional state
A daily mood tracking system using Anxiety / Calm / Happy states, displayed in a calendar and timeline. Owners see feeding patterns, playtime, and rest in detailed hourly breakdowns — letting them spot behavioural changes early.
Pet Sitting
Trusted caregivers, not strangers
Search and filter local pet sitting services — house sitting, boarding, pet hospital, bathing — by distance and ratings. Each listing shows facility photos, transparent pricing, and verified reviews to build the trust that Grace's persona needed most.
Community
You're not alone in this
A supportive space where pet owners share experiences, daily care tips, and connect with others facing similar challenges. Includes "Pawsitive Tips" (curated knowledge), emotional support prompts, and a comment system — normalising the conversation around separation anxiety.

Design system

Warm, legible, emotionally resonant

The visual system was built to feel warm and trustworthy — qualities that matter when users are in an anxious emotional state. Open Sans provides clarity at all sizes. The golden primary palette signals warmth and positivity, while dedicated mood colours (purple for anxiety, green for calm, orange for happy) create an intuitive emotional vocabulary across the app.

What I learned

1
Modern pet ownership is deeply emotional. Many owners — especially young urban professionals — treat their pets as family members and feel genuine guilt when separated. Design that acknowledges this emotional reality, rather than treating it as irrational, creates far more meaningful products.
2
Single pet owners face a specific set of challenges that generic pet apps ignore: no family backup, no one to check in on the pet, heightened anxiety during work trips. Designing for this underserved group revealed how much room exists in the market for emotionally intelligent pet care tools.
3
Community features are underrated in wellness products. Building a space where seeking help for separation anxiety becomes normalised — not embarrassing — was one of the most impactful design decisions in the project. Shared experience reduces isolation.
